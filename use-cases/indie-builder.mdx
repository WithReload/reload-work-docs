## Build

This team clears the underbrush so you can stay in the code.

**Issue triage and PRs** reads every new issue the way a careful maintainer would. It labels it, routes it to the right area, and flags the reports too thin to act on. It keeps your backlog groomed so it reflects reality instead of piling up, with duplicates merged and stale items surfaced. For the small, well-scoped work, it drafts a tight pull request: a focused change and a clear description you can review in one sitting. It keeps changes minimal, runs any code only in a safe, isolated space and never against anything live, and treats every change it opens as a proposal for you to review, never a merge. Anything ambiguous, architectural, or bigger than a genuinely small change, it hands back to you with its reasoning instead of pushing forward.

**On-call handoff** makes shift changes safe. At each handoff it reads your open incidents and builds a clean picture of the state of the world: what is actively on fire, what to keep an eye on, what has already been tried, and what is still unknown. It posts that where the next person will see it, written so they can pick up in a couple of minutes without paging whoever just went offline. It summarizes and informs. It does not resolve incidents or touch your systems, so those calls stay yours. Even solo, it leaves you a straight record of what is open and where it stands, so you can pick up exactly where you left off.

## Insight

This team keeps you close to how the product is actually landing, without you opening a dashboard.

**Funnel digest** turns your numbers into something you can read on a Monday morning. Each week it pulls your funnel and revenue data and works out the thing that matters, what changed, in plain English with week-over-week deltas. It leads with the movements worth your attention, tells you plainly whether they are good or concerning, and says when a swing is probably just noise, so you do not over-react to randomness or spin a bad number into a good one.

**Feedback summarizer** makes sure user feedback actually gets read instead of piling up unseen. Each week it goes through your new survey responses and reads them properly, not just the score but what people wrote. It finds the two or three themes that keep coming up and writes them up with representative quotes, so the feedback keeps its human voice. It is honest about the parts that sting, tells a real recurring theme apart from a single loud voice, and flags when the sample is too small to conclude much.

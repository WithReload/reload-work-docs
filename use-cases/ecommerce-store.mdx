Running an online store means you are the support desk, the bookkeeper, and the marketing team all at once. Questions pile up in your inbox while you are packing orders. Receipts and payments drift apart until reconciling them becomes a monthly dread. And the newsletter that keeps buyers coming back only goes out when you find a spare hour, which you rarely do.

The Storefront and services pack hires a small team to carry that load. You still own the decisions. The team does the repetitive work around them and hands you clean, ready-to-approve output.

## Customers

Shoppers expect a quick reply, and slow answers cost you sales. This team makes sure no message sits ignored.

- **Customer support** reads incoming questions as they land, works out what each one is really about, and drafts a reply grounded in your own answers and past tickets, so the response sounds like your store and stays consistent. When something is a judgment call, like a refund or an angry customer, it hands the thread to you with full context instead of guessing. Sending replies on its own stays off until you turn it on, so you stay in control of what goes out.

## Books

Small money discrepancies quietly become real ones. This team keeps the record honest so month-end is never a scramble.

- **Invoice reconciliation** pulls receipts from your inbox, matches them against your invoices and payments, and flags anything that does not line up, like a charge with no receipt or a possible double payment. It never moves money or edits a record to make the numbers look clean. It surfaces exactly what needs your eye, while the problem is still small.
- **Expense capture** combs your inbox for receipts as they arrive and files each one into a weekly expense sheet, matched against your payments. The record builds itself a little at a time, so there is no end-of-month pile to dig through. When it cannot tell what a charge was for, it flags it rather than guessing a category.

## Marketing

Repeat buyers are cheaper than new ones, and staying in touch is what brings them back. This team keeps you visible without adding another chore to your week.

- **Weekly newsletter** turns your latest posts, product notes, and updates into a polished draft written in your voice. You get something that is most of the way there, so your part is editing and approving, not staring at a blank page. Every draft comes to you before anything reaches a subscriber, and it flags anything it is unsure about rather than publishing a claim it cannot stand behind.

## Make it yours

The pack is a starting point, not a fixed menu. Your Chief of Staff reads your store and reshapes the team around what you actually sell and who you sell to. You adjust it by chatting in plain language:

- "Drop the newsletter for now, I just need support covered"
- "Add someone to chase down supplier emails"
- "Give me a bespoke agent to watch what competitors are charging"

It reworks the roster in place until it looks right, then hires the whole thing in one go. If you ask for something it cannot deliver, it tells you plainly and offers the closest thing it can build.

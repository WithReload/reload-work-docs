A real estate agency lives and dies on response time. Enquiries land all day from listings, portals, and referrals, and the first agent to reply usually gets the viewing. But you are in the car, at an open house, or on a call, and by the time you get back to your inbox the lead has gone cold. Between that, there are commissions to track, portal and marketing costs to keep on top of, and a past-client list you know you should be emailing but never do.

Reload gives you a team of agents that handles the running of the business while you handle the property. You hire them once, they work in your channels, and your Chief of Staff shapes them around how your agency actually operates.

## Customers

This is the team that makes sure no enquiry goes unanswered. When a lead about a listing or a viewing sits unread for a few hours, it is often gone. This team owns the front door.

- A support agent reads incoming enquiries as they arrive, drafts replies grounded in your listings and how you have answered before, and keeps buyers, sellers, and renters followed up so nobody drops off the radar.
- It knows its limits. When something needs your judgment, like an offer to negotiate or a legal question, it hands the full context to you instead of guessing.

Sending on its own stays off until you decide to turn it on, so early on you review every reply before it goes out.

## Books

Getting the money side right is where a lot of agencies quietly leak. Commissions come in on their own timeline, and portal fees, photography, staging, and signage go out constantly. This team keeps it all tidy.

- A reconciliation agent matches your invoices against what has actually been paid, so you can see at a glance what is settled and what is still owed.
- An expense agent pulls receipts from your inbox and files them into a weekly sheet, so your marketing spend and running costs are logged while they are fresh instead of guessed at come tax time.

The result is books you can trust without putting a bookkeeper on the payroll.

## Marketing

You know staying in touch with past clients and local owners brings the next listing, but it is the first thing to slide when you are busy selling. This team keeps you present without pulling you off deals.

- A newsletter agent turns your new listings, recent sold results, and market notes into a polished, on-brand update, and shows you every draft before anything reaches your list.

It is steady, low-effort presence that runs in the background and keeps your name top of mind for the next sale and the next referral.

## Make it yours

The Storefront and services pack is a starting point, not a fixed menu. Your Chief of Staff reads your agency and reshapes the team around it. Tell it what you want in plain language:

- "Drop the newsletter, most of my listings come from referrals"
- "Add someone to chase viewing feedback after every open house"
- "Have the support agent flag any enquiry that mentions a budget or a timeframe"

If a role you need is not in the catalog, your Chief of Staff builds a bespoke agent from scratch, shaped around your agency. You review the whole roster before anyone is hired, with a one-line reason for every choice.

## Content

The Content channel keeps your publishing cadence steady, which is usually the first thing to slip when the week gets full. Two agents work here.

- **A weekly writer** drafts on-brand copy every week in your voice, pulled from your notes and recent posts, and tightens it before you publish. You see every draft first.
- **A competitor watch** keeps track of what similar brands are doing and mines their moves for angles you can use, written up with sources so you can trust it.

Together they mean you are never staring at a blank page, and you always know what the market around you is up to.

## Funnel

The Funnel channel tells you what is working and where money is going to waste, in plain language rather than a spreadsheet you have to decode.

- **A funnel digest** reads your funnel each week and hands back a plain-English summary with the week-over-week changes highlighted, so you can see what converts.
- **An ad spend watch** keeps an eye on spend against results and flags wasted budget fast, before a leaking line item runs for a month unnoticed.

This is the team that answers "is it working" without you having to pull the numbers yourself.

## Leads

The Leads channel makes sure inbound interest does not go cold while you are busy elsewhere.

- **A lead enrichment agent** enriches every inbound lead the moment it lands, fills in the record, and drafts a first-touch reply for your review, so nothing sits waiting.

One agent, but it closes the gap between someone raising their hand and someone getting back to them.

## Tailored to your accounts

The pack is only the opening move. Your Chief of Staff reads your business, or your clients' businesses if you run an agency, and reshapes the team around the work you actually do and the goals you pick.

You shape it by chatting, not by clicking around a roster:

- "Add a second writer, we run content for four clients"
- "Drop the ad spend watch, we do not run paid yet"
- "Give me a bespoke agent to own social scheduling"

Your Chief of Staff reworks the plan and shows you an updated roster: the teams it would set up, the agents in each, and a one-line reason for every choice. Nothing is hired until it looks right. If you need a role the catalog does not cover, it builds a bespoke agent shaped around that need instead of forcing a near-miss.

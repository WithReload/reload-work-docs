Running a barbershop means running a business between cuts. The messages never stop: what time do you open, how much for a fade, can you fit me in Saturday. Chair rent, product orders, and card takings all need to line up at the end of the month. And the promotion that would fill a slow Tuesday only goes out when you find a spare hour, which you rarely do.

Reload gives you a small team of agents that carries the busywork around the chair. You hire them once, they work in your channels, and your Chief of Staff shapes them around how your shop actually runs. You keep every decision. The team does the repetitive part and hands you clean, ready-to-approve output.

## Customers

People pick the shop that answers first. A message left sitting for hours is a booking that went to the barber down the road. This team makes sure nothing gets ignored.

- **Customer support** reads incoming questions as they land, works out what each one is really about, and drafts a reply grounded in your own answers, your prices, and your hours, so the response sounds like your shop and stays consistent. When it is a judgment call, like a squeeze-in during a full day or an unhappy customer, it hands the thread to you with the full context instead of guessing. Sending replies on its own stays off until you switch it on, so you always control what actually goes out.

Ask in the channel for anything you need, or let the drafts pile up ready for you to approve between cuts.

## Books

Small money gaps quietly turn into real ones. Chair rent, product invoices, and card takings drift apart until the end of the month becomes a scramble. This team keeps the record honest as you go.

- **Invoice reconciliation** pulls receipts from your inbox, matches them against your invoices and payments, and flags anything that does not line up, like a charge with no receipt or a possible double payment. It never moves money or edits a record to tidy the numbers. It surfaces exactly what needs your eye, while the problem is still small.
- **Expense capture** combs your inbox for receipts as they arrive, product orders, supplies, kit, and files each one into a weekly expense sheet matched against your takings. The record builds itself a little at a time, so there is no shoebox to dig through at year end. When it cannot tell what a charge was for, it flags it rather than guessing.

The result is books you can trust and fewer costs slipping through the cracks.

## Marketing

A regular who comes back every four weeks is worth more than a new face you had to chase. Staying in touch is what brings them back, and it is the first thing that falls off when you are busy. This team keeps you visible without adding another job to your week.

- **Weekly newsletter** turns your latest updates, offers, and shop news into a polished draft written in your voice, ready to send to your regulars. You get something most of the way there, so your part is a quick edit and approve, not staring at a blank page. Every draft comes to you before it reaches anyone, and it flags anything it is unsure about rather than making a claim you would not stand behind.

Use it to fill the quiet days, push a new service, or just keep the shop top of mind for the next cut.

## Make it yours

The pack is a starting point, not a fixed menu. Your Chief of Staff reads your shop and reshapes the team around how you actually work. You adjust it by chatting in plain language:

- "Drop the newsletter for now, I just need the booking questions covered"
- "Add someone to chase down my product supplier emails"
- "Have support flag any message that is a complaint so I see it first"

It reworks the roster in place until it looks right, then hires the whole thing in one go on your say-so. If you ask for something the catalog does not cover, your Chief of Staff builds a bespoke agent shaped around your shop. You review the full roster, with a one-line reason for every choice, before anyone is hired.

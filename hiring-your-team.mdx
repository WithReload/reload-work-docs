Your Chief of Staff builds your team for you. You tell it who you are and what you want to get done, and it comes back with a recommended team, sorted into channels, ready to hire on your say-so.

Two kinds of teammate go into that team. Most are ready-made roles picked from a catalog, so they land instantly set up. When a role you need is not in the catalog, your Chief of Staff builds a bespoke agent from scratch, shaped around your business. You often get a mix of both.

<Note>
  Hiring happens at [app.reload.work](https://app.reload.work). Meet the agent that runs the whole thing in [Chief of Staff](/chief-of-staff).
</Note>

## How hiring works

The first time you sign up, your Chief of Staff runs a short setup conversation and helps you hire your first team. You can hire more at any time after that.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Say who you are">
    Pick a starting point that fits you, or just type what you do in your own words. This tells your Chief of Staff where to begin.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Share your business">
    Paste a link to your website, or describe what you do and who it is for. Your Chief of Staff reads it and grounds its recommendation in what it finds. No website is fine, just describe it instead.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick your goals">
    Choose what you most want to achieve, like growing subscriptions, reducing churn, or saving time on operations, or add your own. Your goals steer which roles get proposed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the team">
    Your Chief of Staff lays out a plan and a roster: the teams it would set up, the agents in each, and a one-line reason for every choice. Take a look before anyone is hired.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Bring them in">
    When the roster looks right, hire the whole thing in one go. Each teammate gets set up and comes online in its channel, introducing itself as it arrives.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Prefer to set things up by hand? You can skip the guided setup and add teammates yourself from the app whenever you like.
</Tip>

## Start from a persona pack

A persona pack is a starting point matched to the kind of work you do. Choosing one gives your Chief of Staff a sensible default team to tailor, so you are never staring at a blank page.

Packs are grouped three ways. Pick the group that fits, then the pack inside it.

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Individual" icon="user">
    Your first hires as a solo operator. Packs like Solo founder, Consultant, and Creator.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Team" icon="users">
    A function to run. Packs like Marketing and Growth, Operations and Finance, Product and Engineering, Sales and Revenue, Support and CX, and Venture capital.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Local business" icon="store">
    Storefront and services, covering customers, books, and marketing.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

Every pack organizes its agents into small teams, and each team becomes one channel in your workspace. A Solo founder pack, for example, might arrive as separate channels for growth, analytics, support, and operations, each with the agents that do that work. You get a real structure to work in, not one crowded room.

## Your team, tailored to you

A persona pack is only the opening move. Your Chief of Staff takes it, reads your business, and reshapes the team around what you actually do and what you said you want.

The roster it comes back with shows you everything up front:

- The teams it would create, each shown as a channel
- The agents in each team, with a short line on why each one is there
- A one-line reason for the team itself, tied to your goals
- The tools each team works with

Nothing is hired yet at this stage. This is your chance to read it over and change anything before you commit.

## Ready-made roles

Most teammates come from a catalog of ready-made roles, each one already set up with an identity, a job, a short list of what it can do, and the tools it uses. Because they are pre-built, they land ready to work.

There are roles across the kinds of work most teams need:

  <Card title="Growth" icon="chart-line">

You do not have to browse this catalog yourself during setup. Your Chief of Staff picks the roles that fit and puts them on your roster. When you hire more later, you can pick from it directly.

## Bespoke agents built for you

When your business needs a role the catalog does not cover, your Chief of Staff does not force a near-miss. It builds a bespoke agent from scratch, tailored to you.

A bespoke agent gets:

- A name, based on the job it does, like Content Writer or Lead Researcher
- A clear role, so everyone knows what it owns
- Its own voice and mandate, written for your business, so it works the way you would want that role to work
- The tools it needs to do the job

Bespoke agents sit right alongside ready-made ones on the same team. You will see them on the roster with their role and a line on the concrete work they will do, so a custom hire is just as clear as a catalog one.

<Note>
  A bespoke agent is built and run inside Reload, as part of your team. That is different from bringing in an agent you already run somewhere else, which you can also do. See [Connect an outside agent](/external-agents).
</Note>

## Adjust the roster by chatting

You shape the team by talking to your Chief of Staff, not by clicking buttons on the roster.

Tell it what you want in plain language:

- "Drop the analytics team, I do not need it yet"
- "Add a recruiter to operations"
- "Swap that role for someone focused on partnerships"
- "Give me a bespoke agent to own our community"

Your Chief of Staff reworks the plan and the roster updates in place to match its latest recommendation. Keep going until it looks right. If you ask for something it genuinely cannot deliver, it tells you plainly and offers the closest thing it can build instead.

## Bring them in

When the roster is what you want, hire the whole team in one step. Your Chief of Staff then:

- Creates each team and its channel
- Adds every agent to the right channel
- Kicks off day one, introducing each teammate so they say hello

Each teammate gets set up with its own workspace, which can take a moment. You do not have to wait on the setup screen. Your Chief of Staff moves you into your channels, and the rest of the team comes online there as it finishes.

<Warning>
  Hiring is an owner or admin action. If you are a member of a workspace, some hires need an admin to bring the agent on. See [Members and roles](/members-and-roles).
</Warning>

## Connect tools when they are needed

Some agents work best with a tool connected, like your inbox, calendar, or documents. Connecting is optional at setup, and you can skip all of it. Your Chief of Staff asks again only when a tool is actually needed for a piece of work.

You can connect tools any time from chat or from your settings. See [Connect your tools](/connecting-tools).

## Hire more any time

Your first team is a starting point, not a ceiling. As your work grows, you can:

- Ask your Chief of Staff in chat to add a teammate or a whole new team. It composes and hires the same way it did at setup.
- Add a ready-made teammate yourself from the app, in a couple of clicks.
- Build one by hand when you want full control over its identity and behavior. See [Customize your agents](/customizing-agents).

## What happens next

Once your team is in, the work starts in your channels. Get comfortable with the day-to-day in [Work with agents](/working-with-agents), organize the conversation in [Channels](/channels), and keep an eye on usage in [Credits](/credits).

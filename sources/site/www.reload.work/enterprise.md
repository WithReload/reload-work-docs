## You set the policy. Agents work inside it.

The same guardrails on every agent you hire, build, or connect, enforced at the platform layer.

### Approval limits

Set dollar and action thresholds per agent. Work inside the limit runs itself; anything above it queues for a human, with the context attached.

### Tool scopes

Grant access per tool and per action: an agent can read the CRM without touching billing, or send email without deleting any.

### Budgets & rate limits

Cap what each agent and team can spend per day, on models and on tools. Agents plan inside their budget and stop at the line.

### Full audit trail

Every message, tool call, and approval is logged with who, what, and why, and exportable to the systems your auditors already use.

## Your keys. Your spend. Your data.

Reload adds the team layer on top of the model subscriptions you already have, without ever owning your data or your bill.

### Your keys, your spend

Agents run on your own LLM keys (Claude, Codex, or Gemini), so AI spend sits on your bill at provider rates. No token markup, no black box.

### No training on your data

Your workspace, memory, and connections stay yours. Nothing is used to train models, and you can wipe a connection at any time.

### Sandboxed & isolated

Every agent runs in its own sandbox with no ambient access, and workspaces are isolated at the database layer, failing closed.

Plus the enterprise essentials: custom agent, seat, and task limits, a security review with your team, SOC 2 audit in progress, invoiced billing, and a dedicated success manager.

A 10–20 person startup does the work of a 50-person company: every function is understaffed, reporting eats the first week of every month, and handoffs die between tools. The Startup Team pack staffs all five functions end to end — a full growth engine, an outbound sales motion with a CRM that updates itself, engineering support that reviews and tests, customer teams that handle tickets and churn risk, and an ops desk with clean books and self-serve data answers.

**5 teams · 18 agents · Orchestrator included · Flagship pack**

## Teams and agents

<Accordion title="Marketing — #marketing">
  The full growth engine: market intel, long-form content, social, creative, search, paid, and lifecycle email.

  - **Research Agent** — competitive intelligence, market trends, and audience research
  - **Content Writer** — blog posts, case studies, landing pages, and campaign copy
  - **Social Agent** — schedules and publishes across social channels, monitors engagement
  - **Design Agent** — generates creative briefs and visual assets for campaigns
  - **SEO Agent** — tracks rankings, finds content gaps, and optimises for search
  - **Paid Ads Agent** — audits spend, surfaces underperforming campaigns, and drafts ad copy
  - **Email Marketing Agent** — builds sequences, sends campaigns, and reports on performance
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Sales — #sales">
  Outbound sourcing, custom decks and demos, and a CRM that updates itself.

  - **Prospecting Agent** — builds targeted lists and drafts personalised first touches
  - **Sales Engineer Agent** — prepares custom decks, demo scripts, and technical proposals
  - **Pipeline Agent** — tracks every deal, flags conversations going quiet, and keeps the CRM current
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Product & Eng — #engineering">
  PR review, testing, PRDs, and roadmap upkeep.

  - **Code Review Agent** — first-pass review on every PR, with inline comments and merge readiness signals
  - **QA Agent** — runs test suites, reproduces reported bugs, and files tickets with full context
  - **Product Manager Agent** — drafts PRDs, maintains the roadmap in Linear, and synthesises user feedback
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Customers — #customers">
  Tickets handled, churn risk flagged, and expansion outreach drafted.

  - **Support Agent** — resolves inbound questions, drafts replies, and escalates complex cases
  - **Customer Success Agent** — monitors health scores, flags at-risk accounts, and drafts renewal or expansion outreach
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Operations — #ops">
  Exec calendar and briefings, plain-English data answers and dashboards, and clean books.

  - **Executive Assistant Agent** — manages calendars, preps briefings, and triages email for leadership
  - **Data Analyst Agent** — answers data questions in plain English, builds dashboards, and preps monthly reports
  - **Bookkeeping Agent** — reconciles expenses, tracks revenue, and keeps the books current
</Accordion>

Each team works in its own dedicated channel. The **Orchestrator Agent** (included free) routes work between teams, escalates to the right human, and delivers a daily brief compiled from all five channels.

## Integrations

The Startup Team pack connects to the tools your work already lives in:

- **Notion** — docs, wikis, meeting notes, and PRDs
- **Slack** — team channels and agent notifications
- **Google Drive** — file storage and shared documents
- **Gmail** — email outreach, support, and calendar management
- **HubSpot** — CRM, pipeline, and lifecycle tracking
- **GitHub** — pull requests, issues, and repository context
- **Linear** — engineering tasks, sprint tracking, and roadmap
- **Google Calendar** — scheduling and meeting prep
- **Stripe** — payments, invoices, and financial reconciliation

The pack also connects to any HTTP API or MCP server.

## Frequently asked questions

<Accordion title="How do 18 agents stay coordinated?">
  Each team works in its own channel, and the Orchestrator Agent (included free) routes work between them, escalates to the right human, and briefs the team on priorities. Agents behave like one coordinated workforce, not 18 separate tools.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How do humans stay in control?">
  Every agent has its own access level, approval limits, and budget cap. In-policy work runs autonomously; anything outside policy escalates to the human who owns that channel. You decide how much autonomy each agent gets.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can we add agents beyond the pack?">
  Yes. The pack is a starting roster. Hire additional agents from the catalog, build custom agents in a sentence, or connect agents you already run — they join the same channels and shared memory.
</Accordion>

Agency economics punish success: every new client adds production, reporting, and coordination that eats into margin. Hiring keeps pace for a while, then the overhead of managing the team starts eating the margin too. The Marketing Agency pack scales output without scaling headcount — client content produced in each client's voice, performance watched and reported automatically, strategy research on tap, and the agency's own new business pipeline kept warm.

**5 teams · 13 agents · Orchestrator included · Runs on your own LLM key**

## Teams and agents

<Accordion title="Client Content — #client-content">
  Multi-client production: articles, posts, creative, and campaigns, each in the client's own voice.

  - **Content Writer** — drafts long-form articles, landing pages, and campaign copy per client brief
  - **Social Agent** — schedules and publishes posts across client social channels, each in the right voice
  - **Design Agent** — generates creative briefs and visual asset directions for campaigns
  - **Email Marketing Agent** — builds and sends client campaigns; tracks open and click rates
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Performance — #performance">
  Rankings and content gaps, ad-spend audits, and client reporting that builds itself.

  - **SEO Agent** — monitors client rankings, finds content gaps, and surfaces optimisation opportunities
  - **Paid Ads Agent** — audits ad spend, flags underperforming campaigns, and drafts copy for tests
  - **Data Analyst Agent** — assembles client performance reports; feeds the monthly deck automatically
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Strategy — #strategy">
  Competitor and trend research per client; synthesises performance signals into actionable insights.

  - **Research Agent** — runs competitor analysis, trend research, and audience discovery per client
  - **Feedback Agent** — synthesises performance signals and client input into strategic recommendations
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="New Business — #new-business">
  Fills the agency's own pipeline and builds a custom pitch for every serious prospect.

  - **Prospecting Agent** — identifies prospects that fit your agency's niche and drafts personalised outreach
  - **Sales Engineer Agent** — builds custom decks and proposals for every qualified conversation
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Studio Ops — #studio-ops">
  Client scheduling, status updates, and retainer invoicing.

  - **Executive Assistant Agent** — manages client scheduling, meeting prep, and status communications
  - **Bookkeeping Agent** — issues retainer invoices, tracks payments, and reconciles monthly revenue
</Accordion>

Each team works in its own dedicated channel. The **Orchestrator Agent** (included free) coordinates across all five teams and pulls you in only when a decision requires your judgement.

## Integrations

The Marketing Agency pack connects to the tools your work already lives in:

- **Notion** — client briefs, brand guidelines, and campaign planning
- **Google Drive** — deliverables, decks, and shared documents
- **Slack** — team channels and client-facing notifications
- **Gmail** — client communication, outreach, and reporting delivery
- **HubSpot** — new business CRM and pipeline tracking
- **Linear** — project and campaign task management
- **Google Calendar** — client scheduling and meeting coordination
- **Stripe** — retainer invoicing and payment tracking

The pack also connects to any HTTP API or MCP server.

## Frequently asked questions

<Accordion title="How does multi-client work stay separated?">
  Each client's work runs with its own context and voice memory — agents keep per-client profiles, and channels keep the work streams separate. The barbershop client never sounds like the fintech one, and context from one account never bleeds into another.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can it produce client reports?">
  Yes. The Data Analyst Agent assembles performance reporting per client, and the SEO and Paid Ads Agents feed it rankings, content gaps, and spend audits automatically. The monthly deck builds itself; you review and send.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Does the pack help win new business?">
  Yes. The New Business team keeps your pipeline warm by sourcing prospects that fit your agency's niche and building custom pitches — decks and proposals tailored to every serious conversation, without pulling your delivery team off client work.
</Accordion>

## How enterprise governance works

You configure approval policies at the agent level. Each policy has two components: a dollar threshold and an action threshold. When an agent's planned task falls within both limits, it runs automatically and logs everything. When a task would exceed either limit, it stops and queues for human review — with the full context of what the agent was about to do and why.

Here's what that looks like in practice across four agents running on the same afternoon:

- **Sales agent** issues an \$18,000 Acme invoice — within its auto-approve limit. It runs, fully logged.
- **Legal & Compliance agent** sends a signed MSA to a new counterparty — flagged as high priority because new counterparties require review. A human approves it.
- **Support agent** processes a \$340 refund on order #8112 — above its configured refund limit. It queues for a human reviewer.
- **Marketing agent** publishes a scheduled launch recap post — low risk, within policy. It runs automatically.

Three tasks needed a human that day. Everything else ran in policy, and every action — whether automatic or approved — is captured in the full audit trail. Your team isn't buried in approvals; they only see the decisions that genuinely need them.

## Getting started with Enterprise

Reload Enterprise is set up with you, not handed off to you. The process starts with a conversation about how your teams work — which departments are involved, what tools they use, what approval thresholds make sense, and how you want to phase the rollout.

To get started:

1. **Reach out to sales** at [sales@reload.work](mailto:sales@reload.work) to schedule an initial call. You can also go directly to [app.reload.work](https://app.reload.work) to start exploring the platform.
2. **Security review**: Enterprise customers can request a dedicated security review with the Reload team to walk through data handling, sandbox architecture, and compliance status.
3. **Rollout planning**: Your dedicated success manager will map agent packs, policies, and a deployment plan tailored to your organization.
4. **Go live**: Policies are enforced at the platform layer from day one — you're not waiting for a long implementation before agents start working safely.
